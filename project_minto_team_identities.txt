PROJECT MINTO — FICTIONAL TEAM IDENTITY SHEET

Default 17-Team League

1. Brisbane Ironbacks
Colours: Charcoal, burnt orange, white
Identity: A tough inner-city Brisbane club with an industrial, heavyweight feel. The Ironbacks should feel powerful, physical, and hard to beat at home.
Logo direction: Armoured animal or iron-plated back motif, heavy shield shape, bold angular lettering.

2. Gold Coast Stingrays
Colours: Teal, gold, navy
Identity: A fast coastal club built around speed, flair, and attacking football. Their brand should feel sharp, sunny, and dangerous.
Logo direction: Stingray silhouette, wave shapes, sleek modern badge.

3. Moreton Crushers
Colours: Quarry grey, deep maroon, sand
Identity: A South East Queensland club with a rugged working-class identity. The Crushers should feel physical, direct, and grounded.
Logo direction: Cracked stone, hammer/quarry motifs, strong block-style crest.

4. Townsville Cyclones
Colours: Storm blue, silver, white
Identity: A North Queensland club shaped by heat, storms, and regional toughness. The Cyclones should feel explosive and relentless.
Logo direction: Cyclone swirl, lightning/wind motion, aggressive circular badge.

5. Auckland Taniwha
Colours: Black, jade green, silver
Identity: A culturally grounded New Zealand powerhouse with a mythical, intimidating identity. The Taniwha should feel proud, powerful, and traditional.
Logo direction: Taniwha creature head, Maori-inspired patterning used carefully and respectfully, dark shield.

6. Sydney Royals
Colours: Royal blue, gold, white
Identity: A central Sydney glamour club with a polished, big-city feel. The Royals should feel prestigious, technical, and media-heavy.
Logo direction: Crown, lion-free regal badge, clean monogram option.

7. North Sydney Falcons
Colours: Red, navy, white
Identity: A historic-feeling northern Sydney club with a traditional rugby league tone. The Falcons should feel disciplined, sharp, and proud.
Logo direction: Diving falcon, classic shield, heritage-style stripes.

8. Parramatta Stags
Colours: Deep green, gold, cream
Identity: A strong Western Sydney club with a proud suburban identity. The Stags should feel powerful, balanced, and community-driven.
Logo direction: Stag head with antlers, shield crest, strong heritage feel.

9. Penrith Redbacks
Colours: Black, crimson, white
Identity: An outer-west Sydney club with a gritty, aggressive edge. The Redbacks should feel fast, dangerous, and uncompromising.
Logo direction: Redback spider icon, web geometry, sharp modern badge.

10. Cronulla Breakers
Colours: Ocean blue, white, charcoal
Identity: A coastal southern Sydney club with a surf and shoreline identity. The Breakers should feel resilient, mobile, and hard-running.
Logo direction: Breaking wave, coastal crest, clean rounded typography.

11. Macarthur Mustangs
Colours: Forest green, bronze, white
Identity: A south-west Sydney growth-area club with a strong, energetic identity. The Mustangs should feel youthful, fast, and ambitious.
Logo direction: Charging horse, motion lines, modern expansion-style badge.

12. Melbourne Sentinels
Colours: Midnight purple, silver, ice blue
Identity: A southern capital club with a disciplined, professional, defensive identity. The Sentinels should feel organised, clinical, and modern.
Logo direction: Guard helmet, tower/shield motif, symmetrical badge.

13. Canberra Griffins
Colours: Emerald, navy, gold
Identity: A capital city club linked to Canberra’s planned-city identity and Lake Burley Griffin. The Griffins should feel smart, structured, and proud.
Logo direction: Griffin creature, parliamentary/capital hints, formal crest.

14. Perth Firetails
Colours: Red, black, gold
Identity: A Western Australian club with a bold, hot-weather identity based on the red-tailed black cockatoo. The Firetails should feel fast, loud, and expansive.
Logo direction: Cockatoo wing/tail, flame-like feather shape, bold shield.

15. Wellington Orcas
Colours: Black, silver, ocean blue
Identity: A second New Zealand club with a cold, powerful ocean identity. The Orcas should feel tough, intelligent, and defensively strong.
Logo direction: Orca dorsal fin, wave arc, dark ocean badge.

16. Logan Vipers
Colours: Lime green, black, graphite
Identity: A south Brisbane/Logan club with a sharp, dangerous, underdog identity. The Vipers should feel quick, hostile, and hard to contain.
Logo direction: Coiled snake, fang shape, angular badge.

17. Canterbury Ravens
Colours: Black, violet, silver
Identity: A western/inner-south Sydney club with a dark, clever, tactical identity. The Ravens should feel calculating, proud, and defensively awkward.
Logo direction: Raven head or wings, gothic shield, clean dark palette.


Expansion Team Options

18. Cairns Crocs
Colours: Crocodile green, sand, black
Identity: A far North Queensland expansion club with a tropical, physical identity. The Crocs should feel tough, patient, and brutal in contact.
Logo direction: Crocodile head, river/tropical elements, heavy jaw shape.

19. Sunshine Coast Makos
Colours: Aqua, coral, white
Identity: A coastal Queensland expansion club with a bright, fast, attacking identity. The Makos should feel fresh, sharp, and built around speed.
Logo direction: Mako shark silhouette, sun/wave motif, clean modern crest.

20. Christchurch Kea
Colours: Forest green, alpine orange, slate
Identity: A South Island New Zealand club with a clever, energetic identity based on the kea. The Kea should feel smart, chaotic, and fearless.
Logo direction: Kea bird head/wing, alpine shapes, sharp triangular badge.

21. Blacktown Wolves
Colours: Black, steel grey, electric blue
Identity: A Western Sydney expansion club with a hard-edged suburban identity. The Wolves should feel aggressive, loyal, and pack-driven.
Logo direction: Wolf head, moon/urban edge, bold dark crest.

22. Adelaide Copperheads
Colours: Copper, navy, cream
Identity: A South Australian expansion club with a desert/snake identity. The Copperheads should feel dangerous, composed, and distinctive from the east-coast clubs.
Logo direction: Copperhead snake, copper metallic accents, circular or shield badge.

23. Toowoomba Rangers
Colours: Bottle green, sandstone, white
Identity: A Darling Downs expansion club with a rugged inland Queensland identity. The Rangers should feel disciplined, hard-working, traditional, and physically tough, with a strong connection to Toowoomba’s position near the Great Dividing Range.
Logo direction: A simple crest using mountain-range linework, a bold “TR” monogram, or a ranger-style shield. Optional subtle horse/rural elements, but avoid making it look too cowboy-themed. Keep it clean, grounded, and traditional.



Suggested League Structure

Starting league:
Brisbane Ironbacks, Gold Coast Stingrays, Moreton Crushers, Townsville Cyclones, Auckland Taniwha, Sydney Royals, North Sydney Falcons, Parramatta Stags, Penrith Redbacks, Cronulla Breakers, Macarthur Mustangs, Melbourne Sentinels, Canberra Griffins, Perth Firetails, Wellington Orcas, Logan Vipers, Canterbury Ravens.

Expansion pool:
Cairns Crocs, Sunshine Coast Makos, Christchurch Kea, Blacktown Wolves, Adelaide Copperheads, Toowoomba Rangers.
